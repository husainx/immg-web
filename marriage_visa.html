Marriage Visa
Application

A marriage visa is a permit granted to a foreign national to remain in the Maldives, who is married to a Maldivian national. A foreigner travelling to the Maldives to marry a Maldivian, or a foreigner who is married to a Maldivian and travels to the Maldives to register their marriage in the Maldives, shall be sponsored prior to their arrival.

Marriage Visa Requirements and Process

Quick Navigation

Before Arrival

  • Sponsorship Declaration must be submitted online via IMUGA, the digital platform for immigration services
  • After arrival, the foreigner must complete the legal marriage process and apply for a marriage visa within the given period
  • If the foreigner is already residing in the Maldives, a visa transfer has to be made to Marriage Visa, once the legal procedure for the marriage registration is completed

Documents Required for Marriage Visa

  • Application for Permit Extension Form (IM23) stamped by the family court
  • A color copy of the passport biodata page of the applicant
  • Last arrival stamp page (for applicants who are arriving on sponsorship to register their marriage for the first time)
  • Copy of the last visa page (issued to remain in the Maldives). This is applicable only for applicants who already have an existing visa to remain in the Maldives
  • A color copy of the Marriage Certificate
  • ID card copy of the Maldivian spouse
  • Passport size photo (in accordance with the Maldives Immigration's Photo Standard)
  • Medical health screening report from a Hospital/Clinic authorized by the relevant government authority (required only when applying for the first time)
  • Health insurance (required only when applying for the first time)

Application Process (step-by-step)

  • 1.
    Marriage Visa applications must be submitted online via IMUGA portal
  • 2.
    When the application is processed, submitter will be notified via a text message and will be given an appointment to submit the passport for visa
  • 3.
    A fee of MVR50 will be charged for the visa card

Visa Duration & Extensions

  • Marriage Visa is issued for 1 year for new applications
  • Marriage Visa extensions are issued for up to 5 years
  • Extensions for Marriage Visa can be submitted (via IMUGA portal) with minimum 3 months to expire

Important Notes

  • Marriage visa allows employment with proper work authorization
  • Divorce or separation may affect visa status
  • All foreign documents must be apostilled or legalized
